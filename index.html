Listening: Multiple Choice

Types

  • Choose 1 answer
    • 3 options
  • Choose multiple answers
    • 5 options: Choose 2 answers
    • 6 options (rare): Choose 3 answers

Method

Choosing keywords

  • Choosing keywords is extremely important
    • Helps track the audio
    • Differentiate between answers
  • Choose keywords that:
    • Help understand WHAT is being asked
    • Are unique between answers
    • Capture the "essence" of the idea
  • Pay special attention to nouns, verbs and adjectives

Example

  • According to the speaker, the company
    • A. has been in business longer than most of its competitors.
    • B. arranges holidays to more destinations than its competitors.
    • C. has more customers than its competitors.
  • According to the speaker, the company
    • A. has been in business longer than most of its competitors.
    • B. arranges holidays to more destinations than its competitors.
    • C. has more customers than its competitors.
  • company
    • A. in business longer
    • B. more destinations
    • C. more customers

(Cambridge IELTS 15, Test 1, Listening Part 2)

Avoid falling into traps

  • Often, all options will be mentioned in the audio
    • => Be sure to choose keywords that helps differentiate between options
  • Watch for possible contradiction markers
    • E.g. however, but, although, originally, at first, had thought, you might think, etc.
    • Can appear in both the question as well as the audio.
  • Determine the attitude of the speaker.
    • Is it positive or negative?
    • Are they surprised?
    • Certain or uncertain?
    • Etc.

Examples

  • What are the TWO main reasons why this site has been chosen for the housing development?
    • A. It has suitable geographical features.
    • B. There is easy access to local facilities.
    • C. It has good connections with the airport.
    • D. The land is of little agricultural value.
    • E. It will be convenient for workers.
  • main reasons - chosen
    • A. geographical features
    • B. access - local facilities
    • C. connections - airport
    • D. little agricultural value
    • convenient - workers

(Cambridge IELTS 18, Test 2, Listening Part 2)

  • Which TWO opinions about the Luddites do the students express?
    • A. Their actions were ineffective.
    • B. They are still influtential today.
    • C. They have received unfair criticism.
    • D. They were proved right.
    • E. Their attitude is understandable.
  • opinions - Luddites
    • A. actions - ineffective
    • B. still influtential
    • C. unfair criticism
    • D. proved right
    • E. attitude - understandable

(Cambridge IELTS 18, Test 3, Listening Part 3)
